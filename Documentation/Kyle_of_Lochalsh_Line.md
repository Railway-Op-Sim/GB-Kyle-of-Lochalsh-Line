# Kyle of Lochalsh Line

## Route

The Kyle of Lochalsh line is a single track railway with several passing loops, running from Dingwall near Inverness in the East, to Kyle of Lochalsh on the West coast of Scotland.

None of the line is electrified, and all passenger services are provided by Scotrail using Class 158 DMUs.

Most stations on the route are request stops, although this cannot be accurately represented in RailOS currently.

## Simulation

A timetable representing Wednesday 4th March has been included, with all Scotrail passenger services. Also included is a charter service in each direction for a bit of variety, although this didn't operate in real life.

*Please note: This timetable requires v2.23.0 or later of RailOS to run, as it uses the Minimum Dwell Times feature.*

## Operational Tips

- Platforms for Kyle of Lochalsh are provided in the service descriptions - these should be kept to for successful operation, especially for the charter services.
- Keep an eye on arrival and departure times at passing loops to make sure trains pass in the correct places.
